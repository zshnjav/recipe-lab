## Prep (Mise-en-Place)



1. Season Oxtail — Pat the oxtail pieces (4 lb / 1800 g) completely dry with paper towels, then season evenly with kosher salt (½ tsp / 3 g) and black pepper (½ tsp / 1 g) until all surfaces are coated and the meat feels dry, which will help proper browning later.

2. Prepare Aromatics — Dice the onions (2 cups / 300 g) and crush the garlic cloves (3 cloves / 9 g), keeping them separate so they can be added at the correct stages during cooking.

3. Measure Liquids — Measure the pomegranate juice (¾ cup / 180 g), beef stock (1 cup / 240 g), and water (1 cup / 240 g).

4. Prepare Flavor Base — Measure the tomato paste (¼ cup / 70 g) and set aside the bay leaves (2 / 1 g), peppercorns (1 tsp / 2 g), and rosemary sprig (1 / 2 g) so they are ready to add without delay.

5. Measure Thickener & Finish — Measure the neutral oil (¼ cup / 55 g), butter (1 tbsp / 14 g), flour (1 tbsp / 8 g), and red wine vinegar (2 tsp / 10 g) and keep them near the cooking station for quick use later.



## Execution



1. Brown Oxtail — Set the Ninja Foodi to Sauté High and heat the neutral oil (¼ cup / 55 g) for about 1 minute until shimmering, then add the seasoned oxtail (4 lb / 1800 g) in batches and brown for 8–10 minutes per batch, turning occasionally until all sides are deeply browned and a dark crust forms, then transfer to a plate while the bottom of the pot shows browned fond.

2. Cook Onions — Reduce to Sauté Medium and add the diced onions (2 cups / 300 g) to the rendered fat, cooking for about 5 minutes while stirring occasionally until the onions become soft, translucent, and lightly golden at the edges.

3. Add Garlic — Add the crushed garlic (3 cloves / 9 g) and cook about 30 seconds, stirring constantly until fragrant and just beginning to smell sweet without letting it brown.

4. Caramelize Tomato Paste — Add the tomato paste (¼ cup / 70 g) and cook for about 2 minutes while stirring continuously until the paste darkens to a brick-red color and begins sticking slightly to the pot, indicating proper caramelization.

5. Deglaze — Pour in the pomegranate juice (¾ cup / 180 g) and the beef stock (1 cup / 240 g), scraping the bottom for about 2 minutes until all browned bits dissolve and the liquid smells rich, slightly sweet, and savory.

6. Build Braise — Return the browned oxtail (4 lb / 1800 g) to the pot and add the water (1 cup / 240 g), bay leaves (2 / 1 g), peppercorns (1 tsp / 2 g), rosemary sprig (1 / 2 g), and remaining kosher salt (½ tsp / 3 g), stirring gently until the meat is mostly submerged in the liquid.

7. Pressure Braise — Lock the lid and set the Ninja Foodi to Pressure High for 55 minutes, then allow a natural release for about 15 minutes before releasing the remaining pressure, until the meat is very tender and pulling away from the bone.

8. Reduce Sauce — Remove the oxtail to a plate and set the Foodi to Sauté Medium, simmering the liquid for about 5–8 minutes while skimming visible fat from the surface until the sauce looks darker, cleaner, and slightly thickened.

9. Make Roux — In a small pan melt the butter (1 tbsp / 14 g) over medium heat, whisk in the flour (1 tbsp / 8 g) and cook for about 2 minutes until the roux turns lightly golden and smells nutty.

10. Thicken Sauce — Whisk the roux into the simmering sauce and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce becomes glossy, slightly thick, and coats the back of a spoon.

11. Finish with Acid — Stir in the red wine vinegar (2 tsp / 10 g) and simmer for about 1 minute, then taste and adjust seasoning if needed until the flavor becomes balanced, brighter, and more defined without tasting sour.

12. Serve — Return the oxtail to the sauce or plate the meat and spoon the finished sauce over the top, ensuring the final texture is thick, glossy, savory, slightly tangy, and deeply beefy with no excessive sweetness.





Notes:

- Adding acid at the end keeps pressure-braised dishes from tasting flat because sealed cooking prevents natural reduction.

- A small roux gives better body than reducing alone, which can make pressure-braised sauces taste harsh.

- Pomegranate juice works here because its acidity and tannin mimic red wine without adding bitterness.
