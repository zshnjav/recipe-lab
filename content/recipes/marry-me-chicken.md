## Prep (Mise-en-Place)

1. Prep chicken: Pat the chicken breasts (1.84 lb / 835 g) dry and lightly pound or butterfly the thickest parts until evenly thick (about ¾–1 inch) so they cook evenly.

2. Season flour: Combine the plain flour (⅓ cup / 40 g) with ½ tsp kosher salt (3 g) and ¼ tsp black pepper (0.5 g) on a plate.

3. Prep aromatics: Mince the garlic (4 cloves / 16 g) and roughly chop the sun-dried tomatoes (1 cup / 140 g).

4. Measure ingredients: Measure the dried oregano (1¼ tsp / 3 g), paprika (2 tsp / 5 g), dried basil (1½ tsp / 1.5 g), heavy cream (1 cup / 240 ml / 240 g), chicken stock (½ cup / 120 ml / 120 g), grated parmesan (½ cup / 50 g), and lemon juice (1 tbsp / 15 ml / 15 g).

5. Dredge chicken: Lightly coat the chicken breasts (1.84 lb / 835 g) in the seasoned flour (⅓ cup / 40 g), shaking off excess so only a thin layer remains.

## Execution

1. Heat oil: Heat the olive oil (2 tbsp / 30 ml / 27 g) in a large skillet over medium-high heat for about 1 minute until shimmering.

2. Sear first side: Add the chicken (1.84 lb / 835 g) and cook for 4–6 minutes until golden brown and it releases easily from the pan.

3. Sear second side: Flip and cook for 3–5 minutes until lightly browned but not fully cooked, then remove to a plate.

4. Add aromatics: Reduce heat to medium-low and add the garlic (4 cloves / 16 g), sun-dried tomatoes (1 cup / 140 g), dried oregano (1¼ tsp / 3 g), paprika (2 tsp / 5 g), and dried basil (1½ tsp / 1.5 g).

5. Bloom flavors: Cook for 30–60 seconds until fragrant and the garlic smells sweet, not sharp or burnt.

6. Add liquids: Pour in the heavy cream (1 cup / 240 ml / 240 g) and chicken stock (½ cup / 120 ml / 120 g).

7. Add cheese & seasoning: Stir in the parmesan (½ cup / 50 g), remaining ¾ tsp kosher salt (4 g), and remaining ¼ tsp black pepper (0.5 g).

8. Simmer sauce: Cook for 2–3 minutes until the sauce is smooth, gently bubbling, and slightly thickened.

9. Return chicken: Add the chicken (1.84 lb / 835 g) back to the pan and spoon sauce over the top.

10. Finish cooking: Simmer for 6–10 minutes, turning once, until the chicken reaches 165°F / 74°C and the sauce coats a spoon.

11. Add lemon: Turn off the heat and stir in the lemon juice (1 tbsp / 15 ml / 15 g), letting the sauce rest for 1 minute until glossy.

12. Final taste: Taste and adjust salt or pepper if needed, then serve immediately.


Notes:

- Dried basil should cook briefly in oil to fully release flavor.
- Add lemon off heat to keep the cream sauce smooth.
- If sauce thickens too much, loosen with 1–2 tbsp warm stock.
